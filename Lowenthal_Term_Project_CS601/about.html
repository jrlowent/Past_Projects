Biography

I was born in Southern Maryland, and have lived here most of my life. I currently live about 45 minutes Southeast of Washington, D.C. My father is from outside of Baltimore City, and my mother is from New England. Before I was born, my parents moved down to Southern Maryland due to my father's work. My mom used to be a nurse, but stopped to raise my brother and I. I have one older brother who is also a graduate student in a combined M.D./Ph.D. program, and a family dog named Melody. There is a picture of my dog on my home page.

While growing up, I cycled through many different career aspirations, some of them more realistic than others. For a long time, I wanted to become a professional musician and go to school for music. However, I realized that I would likely need to tour, and the lifestyle of being a touring musician is not for me. I studied psychology during my undergraduate education with the intention of studying cognitive neuroscience later. My career ideas shifted a bit, but still fell within that realm. After graduating, I worked in clinical research for a period of time. However, I realized that that career path was not for me. I took a step back and started to explore programming. Eventually, I fell on web development, and now I am studying exactly that at Boston University.

In my free time, I like to keep music in my life and to stay active. I have been playing drums for almost 16 years, and guitar for almost 13. I also like to lift weights and do cardiovascular exercise. These activities have truly had a positive impact on my life, so I do not plan on giving them up anytime soon. I like to explore new places, go to concerts, do outdoor activities, spend time with friends and family, and watch movies. I also like to spend time learning more programming-related things.
